PAg — Professional Agrologist (BC Institute of Agrologists)

Mapping the UBCO Freshwater Science degree to BCIA’s coursework requirements

Published

May 28, 2026

The designation

PAg = Professional Agrologist, the senior tier of the BC Institute of Agrologists. You first qualify as an Articling Agrologist (AAg) straight from your degree, then complete BCIA’s Articling Program plus two years of agrology-related work experience to bridge to PAg.

Academic requirements (AAg entry)

A four-year accredited B.Sc. with a science focus, where the coursework includes:

  • At least 8 entry-level foundational courses (100/200-level) in the subjects on BCIA’s Academic Worksheet.
  • At least 20 courses in agriculture, natural sciences, or agriculture/resource economics that relate to agrology under the Agrologists Regulation, 2021.
  • At least 8 senior-level courses (drawn from the 20 above) in those same subject areas.

Eligible course categories:

  • Agrology
  • Foundational Natural Science
  • Mathematics or Statistics
  • Economics
  • Communications / Writing
  • Computer Science

How Freshwater Science maps to PAg

The Freshwater Science core already covers most of BCIA’s foundational requirements. The gap-closers are the senior-level course count and a small set of category-specific electives.

What’s already covered

  • Foundations (8 required): Year 1 science (BIOL 116, CHEM 113 or 121/123, MATH 100, PHYS 111/112 or 112/122) plus EESC 205 (Hydrology) and EESC 222 (Geomorphology) cover the foundational natural-science count.
  • Communications / Writing: the program’s 6-credit English / communications block satisfies BCIA’s communications requirement.
  • Mathematics / Statistics: MATH 100 + STAT 230 / GEOG 271 / BIOL 202.

Suggested electives to satisfy the 20-course / 8-senior requirement

From BCIA’s UBCO-specific approved list (June 2025), the most relevant electives for a Freshwater student:

  • EESC: 301, 309, 313, 314, 315, 333, 342, 402, 413, 422, 423, 429, 431, 435
  • GIS / Geography: GISC 380, GISC 381, GEOG 380, GEOG 381
  • Economics: ECON 371, 372
  • Chemistry (advanced): CHEM 301, 302, 317

(See the authoritative BCIA UBCO course list (PDF) for the complete approved list. The pathway documents in this repo at docs/reference/program-pathways/ and docs/reference/advising/ reflect the program lead’s curated picks.)

Sample 4-year plan for the PAg track

Illustrative; verify with the program advisor and current BCIA rules.

NoteYear 1
  • T1: BIOL 116; CHEM 121/123; MATH 100; WRDS/ENGL; elective.
  • T2: BIOL 125; CHEM 123/121; PHYS 111 or 112; elective.
NoteYear 2
  • T1: EESC 205 (Hydrology); STAT 230 / GEOG 271 / BIOL 202; ECON 371 or 372 (example); elective.
  • T2: EESC 222 (Geomorphology); CHEM 211; CHEM 201 or 210 (example); elective.
NoteYear 3
  • T1: EESC 301 (Limnology); EESC 402 or other policy/management upper-level; GISC/GEOG 380/381 (GIS/Remote Sensing).
  • T2: Choose among EESC 333, 413, 422, 429, 431, 435; consider CHEM 301/302 as relevant.
NoteYear 4
  • Round out to ≥20 agrology-related courses with ≥8 at 3rd/4th-year across EESC, BIOL, CHEM, GEOG, GISC, ECON. Add a field or industry project (Directed Studies, Honours Thesis, or co-op term).

Co-op and experience

Co-op terms after Year 2 or Year 3 target utilities, local and regional governments, environmental consulting, and agriculture / watershed NGOs. These count toward the post-degree articling experience required to convert AAg → PAg.

Seat / prerequisite cautions

  • GIS courses (GISC 380/381) often have priority rules for majors. Request permission early.
  • ECON seats fill quickly; plan registration day.
  • Some upper-level EESC courses alternate years (313/413). Coordinate with the program advisor.
