RPBio — Registered Professional Biologist (College of Applied Biologists)

Mapping the UBCO Freshwater Science degree to CAB’s coursework requirements

Published

May 28, 2026

The designation

RPBio = Registered Professional Biologist, granted by the College of Applied Biologists — the regulatory body for applied biology professionals in BC. Entry-level is Biologist in Training (BIT) with the academic credential complete; RPBio follows after validated experience, competencies, and submitted work products.

Academic requirements

A bachelor’s degree (or master’s) with:

  • 25 science courses, 13 in biology.
  • Required core courses across:
    • Communications (typically first-year writing)
    • Chemistry
    • Numeracy (math)
    • Statistics at 2nd-year or higher
    • Applied biology / management
    • Ecology
    • Three of five from: genetics, cell biology, physiology, systematics/taxonomy, evolution
WarningKnown UBCO gap: systematics / taxonomy

UBCO does not currently offer a dedicated undergraduate course in systematics or taxonomy. Students targeting RPBio should either (a) plan to satisfy this requirement via a course taken at another institution and transferred in, or (b) cover the three-of-five via evolution + cell biology + genetics (or + physiology) instead. Confirm with the program advisor and CAB before registering. This gap was identified during the May 2026 QAQC pass against the live UBC Okanagan Academic Calendar.

How Freshwater Science maps to RPBio

The Freshwater Science core already covers most of the chemistry, numeracy, statistics, applied-biology, and ecology requirements. The gap to close is the biology course count (need 13) and the “three-of-five subject areas” requirement.

What’s already covered

  • Communications: first-year writing block (ENGL or program-approved alternatives).
  • Chemistry: CHEM 121/123, 201/210/211, 301 — comfortable coverage.
  • Numeracy: MATH 100/101.
  • Statistics (≥2nd-year): STAT 230 or GEOG 271 or BIOL 202.
  • Ecology / applied biology: BIOL 201 (Ecology & Evolution), BIOL 308 (Population Biology), FWSC/BIOL 375 (Flora & Fauna of Inland Waters).

Sample 4-year plan for the RPBio track

Illustrative; verify with the program advisor and current CAB rules.

NoteYear 1
  • T1: BIOL 116; CHEM 121; MATH 100; ENGL 109/112/114 (writing requirement); elective.
  • T2: BIOL 125; the other of CHEM 121/123; PHYS 111 or 112; elective.
NoteYear 2
  • T1: BIOL 201; STAT 230 / GEOG 271 / BIOL 202 (≥2nd-year stats); EESC 205 (Hydrology); elective.
  • T2: EESC 222 (Geomorphology); CHEM 211; BIOL 200 (Cell); elective.
NoteYear 3
  • T1: EESC 301 (Limnology); FWSC/BIOL 375 (Flora & Fauna of Inland Waters); CHEM 301; elective.
  • T2: BIOL 265 (Genetics); BIOL 356 or 358 (Physiology) or BIO upper-level toward the 13-biology count.
NoteYear 4
  • T1: EESC 402 (Freshwater Resources); pick 1-2 of EESC 309 / 323 / 423 (geochem requirement); BIO upper-level (reaching the 13-BIO total).
  • T2: EESC 342 (Hydrogeology) or EESC 413 (Analytical Methods); BIO upper-level; Directed Studies / Field / Special Topics.

Co-op and experience

Co-op terms after Year 2 (summer start) for 8-12 months, or after Year 3 summer. Target biomonitoring, fisheries, environmental consulting, and government. Experience accumulated during BIT counts toward the RPBio bridge.

Seat / prerequisite cautions

  • Some 3xx BIOL seats prioritise Biology majors. Request permission early.
  • BIOL 200, 265, 356, 358, 371 are the gap-filler electives — plan registration so they aren’t squeezed by required EESC courses.

Professional work products

To convert BIT → RPBio you must submit at least one scientific or risk-assessment report that meets College criteria, plus at least one public-facing communication piece (annotated presentation, briefing note, interpretation document, etc.). Directed Studies, Honours Thesis, and co-op term reports are the natural vehicles for these.
