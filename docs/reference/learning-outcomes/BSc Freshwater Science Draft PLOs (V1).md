**Freshwater Sciences Program Learning Outcomes:**

**Depth and Breadth of Knowledge**

1.  Explore water processes in the context of planetary cycles, surface
    processes and environmental systems.

2.  Characterize human impacts on the environment.

3.  Collect, organize and analyze water and environmental science data
    using appropriate laboratory techniques, fieldwork, computer
    applications and statistical techniques.

**Application of Knowledge**

4.  Critically analyze spatial and temporal data, represent it in
    appropriate graphical formats (i.e., figures, tables, plots) and
    formulate well-reasoned interpretations using spreadsheets and/or
    programming (i.e., R, Python).

5.  Solve problems related to water systems using field and analytical
    techniques, modelling approaches, quantitative reasoning and data
    processing; critically assess how human alteration of freshwaters
    jeopardize those systems and evaluate common trade-offs.

6.  Apply the scientific method and critical thinking by creating
    conceptual models from which testable hypotheses or analyses in
    hydrology are developed to inform solutions.

**Generation of Knowledge**

7.  Acquire, critically assess and summarize relevant water and
    environmental science literature, data sources and spatial resources
    such as maps, images, and remotely sensed data.

8.  Solve problems involving the physical, chemical, and biological
    structure and function of freshwater systems.

9.  Create water-related public policies.

**Communication**

10. Communicate limnology and environmental concepts using formal and
    informal written reports, oral presentations, maps, illustrations,
    and technical posters to various audiences.

11. Write clear, detailed, and comprehensible field notes, laboratory
    notes or computer code documentation.

**Professional Conduct**

12. Demonstrate an understanding of professional ethics and planning
    required to carry out fieldwork including engaging with Indigenous
    Peoples, respect for land access, permissions and Indigenous rights.

13. Engage in safe field and laboratory practices.

14. Work collaboratively in a team environment.
