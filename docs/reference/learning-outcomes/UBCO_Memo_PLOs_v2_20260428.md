# 1. Purpose

To present a refined set of Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs) for the BSc
Freshwater Science, informed by (a) student focus group feedback, (b)
faculty consultation, and (c) benchmarking against sector best practice
and comparator BSc programs at peer institutions.

# 2. Consultation Process

Two consultation activities were conducted to inform this revision:

- Student focus group (Freshwater Science majors): review of v1 PLO
  draft, with directed feedback on clarity, omissions, and policy
  alignment.

- Faculty working session (program-affiliated faculty): review of v1 PLO
  draft with directed feedback on list length, redundancy, and alignment
  with UBC institutional guidance.

# 3. Comments Received

## 3.1 Student Feedback

- Endorsed PLOs 3, 4, 5, and 6 as well-written and reflective of program
  strengths in data analysis, modelling, and applied problem-solving.

- Recommended that PLO 7 explicitly include Indigenous knowledge
  alongside western science literature and data sources, recognizing the
  program\'s regional context within Syilx territory.

- Recommended that PLO 9 extend beyond policy creation to include
  helping policy gain traction with decision-makers, reflecting career
  pathways in policy advocacy and science-to-decision translation.

## 3.2 Faculty Feedback

- The v1 list of 14 PLOs is longer than UBC institutional guidance and
  sector norms (typically 5 to 7).

- Several PLOs overlap (e.g., v1 PLOs 3 and 4 on data analysis; v1 PLOs
  5 and 8 on freshwater problem-solving).

- The Generation of Knowledge category contained outcomes that fit more
  naturally within Application or Communication.

- The list should retain UBC\'s standard four-category structure where
  practical, and use measurable Bloom\'s-aligned action verbs.

# 4. Benchmarking Against Best Practice

Best-practice guidance from Penn State, Washington State University,
CSUSB, SUNY, and U Michigan-Dearborn converges on 5 to 7 PLOs as the
appropriate range for an undergraduate degree program. Lists exceeding
10 are flagged as inhibiting meaningful assessment.

# 5. Refined PLO List (v2)

The list below consolidates 14 outcomes into 8, organized in 4
categories.

## Depth and Breadth of Knowledge

1.  Explain water processes in the context of planetary cycles, surface
    processes, and environmental systems, including human alterations to
    freshwater ecosystems.

2.  Describe the physical, chemical, and biological structure and
    function of freshwater systems.

## Application of Knowledge

3.  Collect, organize, and analyze water and environmental science data
    using appropriate field, laboratory, computational (e.g., R,
    Python), and statistical techniques, and represent results in
    appropriate graphical formats.

4.  Apply the scientific method and quantitative reasoning to develop
    conceptual models, test hypotheses, and evaluate trade-offs in
    freshwater management decisions.

5.  Acquire, critically assess, and synthesize freshwater science
    literature, Indigenous knowledge systems (ways of knowing), data
    sources, and spatial resources (e.g., maps, remotely sensed data) to
    inform interpretation and decision-making.

6.  Develop water-related policy options and communicate them
    effectively to advance uptake by decision-makers.

## Communication

7.  Communicate limnology and environmental concepts to diverse
    audiences through formal written reports, oral presentations,
    technical posters, illustrations, and clear field, laboratory, and
    code documentation.

## Professional Conduct

8.  Demonstrate professional ethics, safe field and laboratory practice,
    effective teamwork, and respectful engagement with Indigenous
    Peoples, lands, and rights-holders.
