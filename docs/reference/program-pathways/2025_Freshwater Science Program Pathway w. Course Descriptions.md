**Year 1 -- Foundations**

**Core Courses**

- **EESC 101 -- Environmental Science\
  Introduction to Earth systems and human impacts on water, energy, and
  nutrient cycles.**

- **EESC 111 -- Earth Science\
  Earth's origin, structure, rocks, minerals, resources, and natural
  hazards.**

- **BIOL 116 -- Biology for Science Majors I\
  Evolution, genetics, cellular processes, foundations of ecology.**

- **CHEM 113 -- Principles of Chemistry II\
  Thermodynamics, kinetics, equilibria in aqueous systems.**

- **MATH 100 + STAT/COSC/DATA elective\
  Foundation in quantitative reasoning and data analysis.**

**Enrichment Ideas**

- Introductory GIS Certificate (e.g., ESRI ArcGIS online) -- builds
  mapmaking and spatial data literacy.

- UBCO summer workshops in basic R or Python for data handling.

- Volunteer with local stewardship groups (e.g., Okanagan Nation
  Alliance, OBWB) for early exposure to applied freshwater work.

**Year 2 -- Building Core Competencies**

**Core Courses**

- **EESC 205 -- Introduction to Hydrology\
  Surface water processes, watershed hydrology.**

- **EESC 212 -- Atmospheric Environments\
  Weather, climate, air pollution, climate change.**

- **EESC 222 -- Geomorphology\
  Landscape processes: erosion, rivers, slopes.**

- **BIOL 201 -- Ecology & Evolution\
  Population, community, and evolutionary biology.**

- **CHEM 211 -- Analytical Chemistry\
  Quantitative chemical analysis, instrumental techniques.**

**\**

**Enrichment Ideas**

- Hydrometric Summer Field Course (OBWB/partner micro-credential in
  development): hands-on training with gauging stations, flow
  measurements, and data logging.

- BC Ministry of Environment/DFO Electrofishing Certification -- a
  field-ready skill required for fisheries jobs.

- Summer Co-op or NSERC USRA -- apply theory in research or applied
  water agency setting.

**Year 3 -- Applied & Integrative Science**

**Core Courses**

- **EESC 301 -- Limnology\
  Physical, chemical, biological processes in lakes.**

- **EESC 309 -- Global Biogeochemistry\
  Nutrient and element cycles at ecosystem and global scales.**

- **EESC 342 -- Hydrogeology\
  Groundwater occurrence, aquifer flow, contamination.**

- **BIOL 375 -- Flora & Fauna of Inland Waters\
  Organisms of lakes and rivers, biomonitoring.**

- **CHEM 301 -- Aqueous Environmental Chemistry\
  Chemical processes in freshwater, contaminant dynamics.**

**Enrichment Ideas**

- Summer Remote Sensing & Mapping Workshop -- UAV (drone)
  photogrammetry, LiDAR, or satellite imagery analysis (Sentinel,
  Landsat).

- Introductory Hydrologic Modelling (Raven or SWAT) short course
  (Waterloo/UBC).

- Field Methods in Aquatic Ecology (through Bamfield Marine Sciences
  Centre or equivalent).

- Internship with an environmental consulting firm (Associated
  Engineering, NHC, etc.) to gain applied mapping and modelling skills.

**\**

**Year 4 -- Specialization & Synthesis**

**Core Courses**

- **EESC 402 -- Freshwater Resources\
  Management and sustainability of freshwater systems.**

- **EESC 413 -- Analytical Methods in Hydrology\
  Advanced data and field techniques.**

- **EESC 423 -- Tracers of Natural Processes\
  Chemical/isotopic tracers in hydrology and ecology.**

- **EESC 435 -- Fluvial Field Techniques\
  Applied river geomorphology, channel surveys.**

- **EESC 436 -- Coastal Geomorphology\
  Coastal systems, sediment transport, shoreline dynamics.**

**Enrichment Ideas**

- Advanced GIS & Spatial Analysis Certificate -- ArcGIS Pro or QGIS.

- Hydrological & Climate Modelling Workshops -- exposure to coupled
  surface/groundwater models, EFN apps.

- Research Thesis (EESC 449 -- Honours) or Directed Studies --
  recommended for grad school or policy work.

- Applied summer school abroad -- e.g., Vienna Hydroinformatics School,
  UNESCO-IHE (Netherlands), or summer field schools in watershed
  management.

**Program Bolstering -- General Recommendations**

- Field Camps & Micro-Credentials: UBCO and OBWB are developing
  Hydrometric Monitoring Micro-Credential (with ONA and industry
  partners).

- Professional Certification Prep: Stream electives toward professional
  designations (RPBio, P.Geo., P.Ag., or P.Eng.) if desired.

- Networking & Conferences: Encourage students to attend CWRA, AGU, or
  Canadian Geophysical Union annual meetings.

- Community Science: Partner with Okanagan stewardship councils for
  applied projects in mapping, water quality, and invasive species
  monitoring.
