Enrichment Ideas

- Introductory GIS Certificate (e.g., ESRI ArcGIS online) – builds mapmaking and spatial data literacy.
- UBCO summer workshops in basic R or Python for data handling.
- Volunteer with local stewardship groups (e.g., Okanagan Nation Alliance, OBWB) for early exposure to applied freshwater work.

Enrichment Ideas

- Hydrometric Summer Field Course (OBWB/partner micro-credential in development): hands-on training with gauging stations, flow measurements, and data logging.
- BC Ministry of Environment/DFO Electrofishing Certification – a field-ready skill required for fisheries jobs.
- Summer Co-op or NSERC USRA – apply theory in research or applied water agency setting.

Enrichment Ideas 

- Advanced GIS & Spatial Analysis Certificate – ArcGIS Pro or QGIS.
- Hydrological & Climate Modelling Workshops – exposure to coupled surface/groundwater models, EFN apps.
- Research Thesis (EESC 449 – Honours) or Directed Studies – recommended for grad school or policy work.
- Applied summer school abroad – e.g., Vienna  Hydroinformatics  School, UNESCO-IHE (Netherlands), or summer field schools in watershed management.

Enrichment Ideas

- Summer Remote Sensing & Mapping Workshop – UAV (drone) photogrammetry, LiDAR, or satellite imagery analysis (Sentinel, Landsat).
- Introductory Hydrologic Modelling (Raven or SWAT) short course (Waterloo/UBC).
- Field Methods in Aquatic Ecology (through Bamfield Marine Sciences Centre or equivalent).
- Internship with an environmental consulting firm (Associated Engineering, NHC, etc.) to gain applied mapping and modelling skills.

Program Bolstering – General Recommendations

- Field Camps & Micro-Credentials: UBCO and OBWB are developing Hydrometric Monitoring Micro-Credential (with ONA and industry partners).
- Professional Certification Prep: Stream electives toward professional designations ( RPBio ,  P.Geo .,  P.Ag ., or P.Eng.) if desired.
- Networking & Conferences: Encourage students to attend CWRA, AGU, or Canadian Geophysical Union annual meetings.
- Community Science: Partner with Okanagan stewardship councils for applied projects in mapping, water quality, and invasive species monitoring.
