students in the Freshwater Science Major at UBC Okanagan, based on the
2025 Calendar. It includes required core courses, electives, and
enrichment opportunities. Students should consult an academic advisor to
finalize individual plans.

# Enrichment Opportunities (Recommended for Summers and Beyond)

\- Hydrometric Monitoring Micro-Credential (UBCO/OBWB)\
- Electrofishing Certification (DFO/Province)\
- GIS and Remote Sensing Workshops (ArcGIS, QGIS, UAV mapping)\
- Co-op placements, NSERC USRA, or Go Global exchange\
- Research projects leading to Honours or graduate school\
- Field schools (e.g., Bamfield Marine Sciences Centre, international
programs)

# Notes

• A minimum of 120 credits is required to graduate, including at least 6
credits of upper-level electives (300+ level).\
• 9 credits of non-science electives are required, and at least 3
credits must satisfy the Indigenous content requirement.\
• Students considering professional designations (RPBio, P.Geo., P.Ag.)
should consult the program advisor early to ensure elective choices
align with requirements.\
• The program roadmap is flexible; course sequencing may shift depending
on offerings and prerequisites.
