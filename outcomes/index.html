Program Learning Outcomes

What a Freshwater Science graduate is expected to be able to do

Published

May 28, 2026

The current authority for Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs) is the v2 PLO set finalised on 28 April 2026 by Dr. Nelson Jatel, PAg, Lecturer and Program Advisor (memo: docs/reference/learning-outcomes/UBCO_Memo_PLOs_v2_20260428.docx). The v2 set consolidated the v1 draft (14 outcomes) down to 8 outcomes in 4 categories, based on:

  • Student focus-group feedback (Freshwater Science majors),
  • Faculty working-session input, and
  • Best-practice benchmarking against Penn State, Washington State, CSUSB, SUNY, U Michigan-Dearborn, and comparator BSc Freshwater programs at UW-Milwaukee, Western Michigan, and UW-Madison.

The 5-to-7-outcome range is the sector norm; v1’s 14 was flagged by both faculty and benchmarks as too long for meaningful assessment.

The eight v2 outcomes

Depth and Breadth of Knowledge

1. Explain water processes in the context of planetary cycles, surface processes, and environmental systems, including human alterations to freshwater ecosystems.

2. Describe the physical, chemical, and biological structure and function of freshwater systems.

Application of Knowledge

3. Collect, organise, and analyse water and environmental science data using appropriate field, laboratory, computational (e.g., R, Python), and statistical techniques, and represent results in appropriate graphical formats.

4. Apply the scientific method and quantitative reasoning to develop conceptual models, test hypotheses, and evaluate trade-offs in freshwater management decisions.

5. Acquire, critically assess, and synthesise freshwater science literature, Indigenous knowledge systems (ways of knowing), data sources, and spatial resources (e.g., maps, remotely sensed data) to inform interpretation and decision-making.

6. Develop water-related policy options and communicate them effectively to advance uptake by decision-makers.

Communication

7. Communicate limnology and environmental concepts to diverse audiences through formal written reports, oral presentations, technical posters, illustrations, and clear field, laboratory, and code documentation.

Professional Conduct

8. Demonstrate professional ethics, safe field and laboratory practice, effective teamwork, and respectful engagement with Indigenous Peoples, lands, and rights-holders.

How each course contributes — the I / R / M methodology

The curriculum-map methodology used to trace these PLOs through the program uses three coding labels for each course-learning-outcome (CLO) → PLO link:

Code Meaning
I Introductory — the course introduces the concept; students encounter it for the first time.
R Reinforcement — the course practises the concept; students extend prior exposure.
M Mastery — the course expects students to demonstrate the concept independently.

Combined with an X marker showing which CLO maps to which PLO, this is the same framework UBC’s Faculty of Science uses for the EECB program (Ecology, Evolution, and Conservation Biology) and other B.Sc. programs.

NotePilot: EESC 301 Limnology

A worked example of the methodology for EESC 301 (Limnology) mapped to the EECB program PLOs is committed at docs/reference/learning-outcomes/EESC 301 - Mapping to EECB Program Learning Outcomes - NJ.xlsx. It captures six course learning outcomes, links each to one or more program PLOs, and codes the depth (I / R / M). This is the template that needs to be replicated for every Freshwater Science required and elective course.
