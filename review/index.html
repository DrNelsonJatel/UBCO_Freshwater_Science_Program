For Department Review

Cover note for the UBCO Freshwater Science planning resource

Published

May 28, 2026

Purpose of this note

This site is a curated advising and planning resource for the Bachelor of Science Freshwater Science major. It exists alongside — not in place of — the UBC Okanagan Academic Calendar, which remains the authoritative source for all degree requirements.

The site is intended for two audiences: current Freshwater Science students (the headline use case is the in-development interactive planner) and prospective students browsing the program.

I am sharing this with the Freshwater Science Program Committee and the Department of Earth, Environmental and Geographic Sciences to invite review of three specific judgement calls embedded in the material. Each is summarised below, with the relevant evidence and the section of the site where the curated content lives.

What I want your input on

1. The v2 Program Learning Outcomes

The site renders the v2 PLO list (eight outcomes in four categories) finalised in the 28 April 2026 memo, which consolidated the v1 draft of 14 outcomes following:

  • a student focus group (Freshwater Science majors),
  • a faculty working session, and
  • best-practice benchmarking (Penn State, WSU, CSUSB, SUNY, U Michigan-Dearborn, plus comparator BSc freshwater programs at UW-Milwaukee, Western Michigan, and UW-Madison).

The v1→v2 crosswalk and benchmarking evidence are in §4 and §6 of the memo, and the eight outcomes themselves are rendered verbatim on the Outcomes page.

Asks:

  • Is the eight-outcome v2 list the final version the Program Committee endorses for institutional submission, or should we iterate?
  • The curriculum-map matrix (per-course I/R/M coding for each PLO) is the next deliverable from the memo. The EESC 301 pilot mapping shows the methodology. We need a path to interview each Freshwater Science instructor (≈ 20-40 hours total). Can the department support this in fall 2026?

2. The RPBio systematics-taxonomy gap

UBCO does not currently offer a dedicated undergraduate course in systematics or taxonomy. This is a real gap for Freshwater Science students targeting Registered Professional Biologist (RPBio) registration with the College of Applied Biology, because the June 2025 CAB Credentialing Standard lists systematics as one of the five subject areas from which RPBio candidates must hold at least three. The RPBio designation page carries a prominent callout-warning surfacing this gap and recommending that students either transfer in a systematics course from another institution or cover the three-of-five via evolution + cell biology + genetics instead.

Asks:

  • Should the department propose a UBCO course to close this gap (e.g., a 300-level “Biodiversity and Systematics” course shared with BIOL or EESC)? If yes, this advising site can become the forcing function for that proposal.
  • In the interim, is the callout-warning on the RPBio page an acceptable disclosure for students considering this designation, or do we want to soften / strengthen the language?

3. The designation pathway recommendations

The site surfaces curated 4-year sample plans for two professional designations:

  • Professional Agrologist (PAg) — BC Institute of Agrologists
  • Registered Professional Biologist (RPBio) — College of Applied Biology

It deliberately does not surface pathways for:

  • Professional Geoscientist (P.Geo) — EGBC. The Environmental Geoscience track is technically viable but cross-loaded with compulsory geoscience courses (mineralogy, sed/strat, structural, geophysics) that compete with the freshwater core. Decision: better signposted as a complementary pathway, not a headline track.
  • Professional Engineer (P.Eng) — EGBC. A B.Sc. degree does not normally meet CEAB engineering academic standards; this is a second-degree path.

Both decisions were made by the program lead based on the advising-pack source document and on what we hear from students. The Complementary pathways page covers the GISC-Minor, Chemistry, Biology, and hydrology/water-resources course bundles that students can layer on top of the FW degree.

Asks:

  • Does the department agree with surfacing only PAg + RPBio as headline designation tracks?
  • The complementary-pathways page suggests three curated electives bundles — water quality + biogeochemistry, aquatic ecology, and hydrology + water resources. Are there additional bundles the department would surface, or wording on these we want to refine?
